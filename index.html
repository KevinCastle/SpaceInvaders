This game is one of the all-time classic video games and we can think about it as the first shooter game of all time and obviously the first one to introduce the fighting aliens!

Space invaders was created by Taito, a small Japanese company founded in 1953. One of its first products was a clone of the famous Pong. Toshihiro Nishikado, the father of Space Invaders, was a programmer working on Taito.

Nishikado presented an idea about a soldier who, behind some trenches, shoots at enemies that were approaching him. But in Taito they thought maybe it could be too violent and it'll not socially accepted (Just remember it was 1978 and the videogames were non violent) and maybe would carry problems to them. After thinking about it, he decided to change it to aliens and spaceships. Months later, Space Invaders was born.

The mechanics couldn't be simpler. Behind green cosmic shields the player has to defeat 5 columns of 11 aliens approaching him faster and faster each time and evading their laser rays, and the player just has 3 lifes to survive. No matter if the mechanics are simple, it results very adictive! The player controls a spaceship in the bottom of the screen and it can only move right or left. A third button allow you to fire straight to the top, if it impacts against an enemy it'll be destroyed. The objective is destroy all of them before they reach you.

Exist three types of enemies:

10 points

20 points

40 points

And a special enemy:

randomly number either 50, 100 or 200 points

The shields that protect the player's spaceship breaks everytime a laser collide it (player laser's breaks it too!), being sometimes an obstacle and giving a bit of difficulty to the game. Through the years the players made strategies to survive longer. One of this strategies is to delete invader's from column to column from one side to the other.

The most important thing about this game is the Hi-Score. The objetive is to look for the best score since space invaders didn't time the game, they only give you three lives to survive.
